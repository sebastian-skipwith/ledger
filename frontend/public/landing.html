The Desktop HUD

A command center for your money,
one keystroke away.

The HUD is a native desktop app for Windows and macOS that floats your finances above every other window — so you never have to go looking for them. Here's everything it does.

It stays on screen while you do everything else. Email, spreadsheets, code, games — the HUD floats above all of it. Drag it anywhere, reshape it to anything, reorder the tiles, or hit Ctrl+Shift+P to make it click-through so it never gets in your way.

▲
Always on top
A slim bar pinned above everything — email, spreadsheets, your IDE, a game. It's just there, all day, without stealing a single pixel of your workflow.
↕
Live tiles & deltas
Net Worth, Safe to Spend, Credit Cards this week, Bills in 7 days, Goal pacing — each tile shows the number and its green/red change, with a 1D / 1W / 1M toggle.
⌨
Global hotkeys
Ctrl+Shift+H shows or hides the HUD instantly. Ctrl+Shift+P flips it into ghost mode. They work from any app, anytime — no need to click the bar first.
◌
Ghost (click-through) mode
Make the bar see-through to your mouse — clicks pass straight through to whatever's behind it. The HUD stays visible but is completely out of the way.
⤢
Reshape to fit
Drag any edge and the layout fluidly reflows — from a full-width top bar to a compact corner widget. It fills whatever size and shape you give it.
⠿
Drag to reorder
Grab any tile and drop it where you want. Put the numbers you care about first — your order is remembered between launches.
⇧
Move it or snap it
Drag the HUD anywhere on screen by its grip, or hit snap-to-top to lock it flush against the top edge of your display in one click.
⚙
Settings panel
Tune bar opacity, choose exactly which metrics show, set the refresh interval, switch light/dark, toggle launch-at-startup, or sign out — all inline, without leaving the bar.
⟳
Starts & updates itself
Opens automatically when your computer boots, refreshes your accounts on a schedule, and updates itself in the background with cryptographically signed releases.
▾
Tuck it away
Minimize the HUD to the system tray when you want a clean screen, and bring it back from the tray menu or a hotkey the moment you need a glance.
